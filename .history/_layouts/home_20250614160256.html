Brief Bio

I am a postdoctoral research fellow at the Center for Cognitive and Brain Health at Northeastern University.

In 2024, I received my Ph.D. in Personal Health Informatics from Northeastern University's Khoury College of Computer Sciences and Bouvé College of Health Sciences. I also hold graduate degrees in Economics from the Barcelona Graduate School of Economics and in Information Science from the University of Michigan.

My research is driven by the goal of extending healthspan and enhancing cognitive performance through technology-enabled interventions. Integrating approaches from computer science, behavioral economics, and information science, I specialize in:

  • mHealth technologies
  • Passive sensing
  • Behavioral modeling
  • Computational mechanistic modeling
  • Decision-making frameworks

These methods help me tackle challenges in health monitoring, cognitive aging, and physical activity interventions across diverse populations.
