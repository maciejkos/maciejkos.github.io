Brief Bio

My name is Maciej Kos.
I am a postdoctoral research fellow at the Center for Cognitive and Brain Health at Northeastern University.

Funding

My current work is funded by the NIH NIA K00 Career Award. Previously, I was funded by ACM/Intel Corporation Computational and Data Sciences Fellowship and Google Scholarship.

Education

  • Ph.D. in Personal Health Informatics, Khoury College of Computer Sciences and Bouvé College of Health Sciences, Northeastern Universitys
  • MA in Information Science, University of Michigan
  • MS in Economics of Science and Innovation, Barcelona School of Economics

My research is driven by the goal of extending healthspan and enhancing cognitive performance through technology-enabled interventions. Integrating approaches from computer science, behavioral economics, and information science, I specialize in:

  • mHealth technologies
  • Passive sensing
  • Behavioral modeling
  • Computational mechanistic modeling
  • Decision-making frameworks

These methods help me tackle challenges in health monitoring, cognitive aging, and physical activity interventions across diverse populations.

Funding and Awards

My work has been recognized and supported by prestigious awards:

  • NIH NIA K00 Career Award
  • ACM/Intel Corporation Computational and Data Sciences Fellowship
  • Google Scholarship

Industry Collaborations

I actively collaborate with industry partners, translating academic insights into practical, user-focused solutions. My goal is to provide interpretable and actionable insights into user behavior that directly impact end-users.

Teaching and Mentoring

I regularly teach and mentor students, finding great satisfaction in helping early-career researchers and industry professionals develop their skills.
