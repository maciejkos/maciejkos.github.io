Brief bio

I am a Ph.D. student in Personal Health Informatics at Northeastern University’s Colleges of Computer and Information Science and Bouvé College of Health Sciences. I am also an ACM/Intel Corporation Computational and Data Sciences fellow.

Having master’s degrees in Economics from Barcelona Graduate School of Economics and in Information Science from the University of Michigan, I apply my interdisciplinary expertise to creating individualized, closed-loop, computational behavior models for novel health technologies, specifically targeting marginalized and underrepresented populations.

I spend most days modeling human behavior in R/Python and working for an IARPA project focused on improving fluid intelligence using games and brain stimulation.
